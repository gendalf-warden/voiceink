Installing VoiceInk

First-time setup, five minutes total.

← Back to download

What to expect: macOS will warn you twice — once because the app was downloaded from the Internet, and once when it asks for permission to listen to your microphone and watch your keyboard. Both are normal. Click through, and you'll be dictating within five minutes.

1Download

On the home page, click Download VoiceInk. Safari (or Chrome / Firefox) may ask you to confirm the download — allow it.

[screenshot: Safari download confirmation dialog]

The file is named VoiceInk-X.Y.ZZZ.dmg (around 13 MB). Open it from your Downloads folder.

2Drag to Applications

The DMG opens a window with the VoiceInk icon on the left and an Applications folder shortcut on the right. Drag the icon onto the Applications folder.

[screenshot: DMG drag-to-Applications window]

Eject the DMG once the copy is done (right-click on it in Finder → Eject).

3First launch — Gatekeeper warning

Open Applications → double-click VoiceInk. macOS will show a warning that the app was downloaded from the Internet:

[screenshot: macOS "downloaded from Internet" dialog with Open button]

Click Open. This warning appears only the first time you launch any app from outside the App Store — even though VoiceInk is signed and notarized by Apple, the system still asks for explicit consent. You won't see it again.

If you see a different dialog saying VoiceInk "cannot be opened because the developer cannot be verified" or "is damaged" — you have an older or unsigned build. Re-download from the home page to get the current notarized version.

4Grant Microphone access

VoiceInk needs your microphone to hear what you're dictating. macOS will ask once:

[screenshot: macOS microphone permission dialog]

Click Allow. If you accidentally clicked Don't Allow, you can fix it later in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone → VoiceInk.

5Grant Accessibility access

VoiceInk needs Accessibility permission to listen for the Fn key from anywhere and to paste text into the focused window. macOS opens System Settings to the right page:

[screenshot: System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility with VoiceInk toggle]

Flip the toggle next to VoiceInk. You may need to enter your password. Close System Settings — VoiceInk picks up the change within two seconds, no restart needed.

6Find the icon in your menu bar

VoiceInk lives in the menu bar (top right of your screen, next to the clock and battery). Look for the microphone icon:

[screenshot: menu bar with VoiceInk microphone icon highlighted]
Don't see it? On MacBook Air and MacBook Pro 14"/16", the camera notch can hide menu bar items. Hold Cmd and drag any menu bar icon to rearrange — drag VoiceInk to a visible position. macOS remembers your placement next time.

7Try dictating

Open any text field — a Slack message, a Notes app, your email draft. Then:

  • Hold the Fn key (bottom-left of your keyboard)
  • Speak naturally — full sentences work best
  • Release Fn

The text appears at the cursor a second later, punctuated and clean. The menu bar icon changes color while recording (blue) and transcribing (purple).

[screenshot: menu bar icon animation states + dictated text in a Notes window]

That's it. The first time you dictate, VoiceInk may take 5-10 seconds to warm up — subsequent dictations are near-instant.

Troubleshooting

Menu bar icon never appears

Check that VoiceInk is actually running: Cmd+Space → type "Activity Monitor" → look for voiceink. If it's there, the icon is just hidden behind the camera notch — see step 6.

If the process isn't running, re-launch from Applications. If it crashes immediately, send us the crash report from ~/Library/Logs/DiagnosticReports/.

Holding Fn doesn't record anything

This means Accessibility permission isn't granted (step 5). Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and confirm the toggle next to VoiceInk is on. If the toggle is already on, turn it off and on again — sometimes macOS needs a nudge.

Dictation works but text doesn't paste

Same as above — Accessibility permission. VoiceInk uses Accessibility to simulate Cmd+V.

First launch downloads ~3.5 GB of models

That's expected. VoiceInk runs everything locally — no cloud — so it needs to bundle the speech-recognition and punctuation models. The download happens once. After that, VoiceInk works offline.

Log files

If something is wrong and you want to share logs: ~/.config/voiceink/voiceink.log
