Installing VoiceInk

First-time setup. About 5 minutes plus the model download.

← Back to download

What to expect. On first launch macOS will ask you to confirm you really want to open the app (it was downloaded from the Internet — standard for anything outside the App Store). Click Open. Then VoiceInk asks for Microphone and Accessibility permissions, then downloads its speech-recognition models (~3.5 GB).

1Download

On the home page, click Download VoiceInk. Your browser may ask you to confirm the download — allow it.

The file is named VoiceInk-X.Y.ZZZ.dmg (around 13 MB). Open it from your Downloads folder.

2Drag to Applications

The DMG opens a window with the VoiceInk icon on the left and the Applications folder shortcut on the right. Drag the icon onto the Applications folder.

When the copy finishes, eject the DMG (right-click on it in Finder → Eject).

3First launch — confirm you want to open it

Open Applications in Finder, then double-click VoiceInk. macOS shows a single confirmation:

Click Open. You won't see this dialog again on this Mac.

Why does macOS ask? Apps that didn't come from the Mac App Store get a one-time confirmation on first launch. VoiceInk is signed by an Apple-issued Developer ID and was notarized by Apple — the dialog itself confirms "Apple checked it for malicious software and none was detected" — so this is just an extra safety prompt, not a warning.

4Welcome to VoiceInk — grant permissions

On first launch VoiceInk shows a welcome window asking for two permissions:

Microphone

Click Grant next to Microphone. macOS will show a standard permission dialog — click Allow. The Microphone row gets a green check.

Accessibility

Click Grant next to Accessibility. macOS shows an explainer dialog:

Click Open System Settings. System Settings opens to Privacy & Security → Accessibility. Find VoiceInk in the list and flip its toggle on:

You may need to enter your password. Close System Settings and return to the VoiceInk welcome window — both rows should now show a green check:

Click Continue.

Why both permissions? Microphone lets VoiceInk hear what you're dictating. Accessibility lets it (a) detect the Fn key from any app you're in and (b) paste the transcribed text into the focused window. Without both, dictation can't work.

5Wait for the models to download

VoiceInk runs everything locally — no cloud — so on first launch it downloads the speech-recognition and punctuation models (~3.5 GB total). Progress is shown:

The download is split into three components:

  • Speech recognition — Whisper large-v3-turbo (~547 MB). Required.
  • GPU acceleration — CoreML encoder (~1.1 GB). Makes transcription 3-4× faster on Apple Silicon.
  • Smart punctuation & translation — Qwen 2.5 LLM (~1.8 GB). Adds punctuation, fixes light grammar, and powers translation mode.

You can keep using your Mac while this downloads. Once complete, VoiceInk is ready and the menu bar icon turns from loading state to the microphone icon.

6Find the icon in your menu bar

VoiceInk lives in the menu bar (top right of your screen, next to the clock and battery). Look for the microphone icon:

Don't see it? On MacBook Air and MacBook Pro 14"/16" the camera notch can hide menu bar items. Hold Cmd and drag any menu bar icon to rearrange — drag VoiceInk to a visible position. macOS remembers your placement on the next launch.

7Try dictating

Open any text field — a Slack message, Notes, an email draft. Then:

  • Hold the Fn key (bottom-left of your keyboard)
  • Speak naturally — full sentences work best
  • Release Fn

The text appears at the cursor a second later, punctuated and clean. The menu bar icon changes color through the pipeline — grey idle, blue while recording, purple while post-processing:

That's it. The first dictation may take 5-10 seconds to warm up — subsequent ones are near-instant.

Troubleshooting

I see "Apple could not verify VoiceInk" with Done / Move to Trash — different from the screenshot

You're probably on an older VoiceInk build (pre-0.5.010) or an older macOS where Gatekeeper was stricter. To bypass:

  1. Click Done — not Move to Trash.
  2. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll to the Security section.
  3. You'll see "VoiceInk" was blocked to protect your Mac with an Open Anyway button. Click it, enter your password.
  4. A second confirmation dialog appears — click Open Anyway again. VoiceInk launches.

To avoid this in the future, download the current version (≥0.5.010) which shows the friendly one-click dialog.

Menu bar icon never appears

Two common causes:

  • Behind the camera notch on MacBook Air / Pro 14" / Pro 16". Hold Cmd and drag any menu bar icon — you'll be able to drag VoiceInk into view.
  • App crashed. Check Activity Monitor (Cmd+Space → "Activity Monitor") for a voiceink process. If it's missing, re-launch from Applications. If it crashes on relaunch, check ~/Library/Logs/DiagnosticReports/ for a crash report and send it to us.
Holding Fn doesn't record anything

Accessibility permission isn't granted. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility and confirm the toggle next to VoiceInk is on. If it's already on, turn it off and on again — sometimes macOS needs a nudge after toggle changes.

Dictation works but text doesn't paste

Same fix as above — Accessibility permission. VoiceInk uses Accessibility to simulate Cmd+V. If the toggle is on but paste still fails, restart VoiceInk from the menu bar (right-click icon → Quit, then re-launch from Applications).

Models download is stuck or failed

The download pulls from GitHub. If your network blocks GitHub or has spotty connectivity, the progress bars stall. Cancel the dialog, check your connection, then re-launch VoiceInk — it resumes from where it left off.

Where are the log files?

~/.config/voiceink/voiceink.log — rotated when it hits 1 MB (previous log saved as voiceink.log.old).
